About the project

The course is organised by Karma Kadjy School, Diamond Way Buddhist organisation of Denmark.

The inauguration of the Copenhagen Victory Stupa is a celebration of the historical moment in 1972, when Hannah and Ole Nydahl first brought the timeless transmission of the Buddhist Karma Kagyu lineage from H.H. the 16th Karmapa, Rangjung Rigpe Dorje to Denmark and then during the next forty-seven years spread it to the West.

About sleeping hall during the course

We will offer a sleeping hall only for three nights during the weekend - on the Friday, Saturday and Sunday nights. We have only 150 places available at the moment, so please if you have a possibility, find your own accomodation in the city.

Registration for the sleeping hall:

  1. Register for the course and pay
  2. You will receive a confirmation email with your registration number
  3. Write an request to stupa.dormitory@gmail.com with your registration number and the names of the registered people
  4. Wait for the confirmation that we have a place for you in the sleeping hall

We are constantly working on finding another sleeping hall. If we find it we will send an update to all those, who registered by the email.

Language and translation:

All lectures will be in English with simultaneous radio translation into several languages.

Please bring your radio with headphones if you need translation.

Parking possibilities:

There are limited parking places near the course place. Therefore we recommend to come to the course by public transport or to find a parking in the remote streets.

Information on parking in Copenhagen in general https://international.kk.dk/artikel/parking-copenhagen

Public transport:

The public transportation in Copenhagen is very reliable, punctual, and it takes you everywhere. In Copenhagen the trains, Metro and busses (including waterbuses) can be accessed with the same ticket. All you need to know is how many zones you will pass on your journey.

Check https://www.journeyplanner.dk/ for transport solutions and tickets.

Closest S-station to the course and meditation place is “Svanemøllen” (1,1 km)

On Friday and Saturday night there are night busses and trains available.

Tickets and prices:

If you have mobile internet, we recommend you to use an app “Mobilbilletter” to buy tickets with your mobile. Otherwise use ticket machines before you enter transport. Bus drivers accept only cash in dkk.

Here you will find all information about tickets and prices: https://www.visitcopenhagen.com/copenhagen/transportation/tickets-prices
